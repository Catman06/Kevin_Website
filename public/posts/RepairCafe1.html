Over the weekend I volunteered at the first Peoria Repair Cafe. It's an event that provides a space for volunteers with knowledge about repairing things to assist and teach people with things that need to be repaired. I, of course, volunteered to work with computers.

In preparation for the Cafe, I gathered whatever I thought might be useful. This included things that made sense, like a multimeter and a wide variety of screwdriver bits, up to far less likely to be used tools, like an old PSU.

When I arrived at the community center where the Repair Cafe was taking place I got set up at the computer repair table and started waiting for the event to start. As I waited for our first computer in need of repair I talked with the other person on computer repair, Ruben.

Our first "repair" was helping a woman set up a few small devices with her phone. There was a portable thermal printer, a security camera, and some hearing aids. With the printer and security camera, it was a case of installing a sketchy app from the app store and connecting the device, and the hearing aids were a standalone device.

After that we got our first actual repair: 2 CD changers. Were they computers? No. Should they have gone to the people doing small appliances? Maybe. But did we take on the job? Yes. Unfortunately, it was an unmitigated failure. We repaired neither of the changers, though we did successfully disassemble and reassemble them.

With the first CD changer, we narrowed the issue to the playback motor, probably. It wasn't spinning at a steady speed, and so it while it could have been something else, it was the only thing I could think of it being in the moment. We did manage to find the part number, however due to it being a ~30 year old device we couldn't find a replacement.

What I should have done is properly researched the device and the issues it was having. Instead I got caught up in what should have been a simple check for any visible damage.

With the second CD changer, it was a similar situation, where we fully focused on disassembly and didn't do proper research on the problem. It's problem was less clear, it would pick up and spin a disc, but wouldn't play it.

In both of these devices cases, It's probably unlikely that there would have been any fix we could have performed. If it was a blown capacitor, I had brought a box of capacitors, if something had become unsoldered, I had a soldering iron. Unfortunately, it probably was something like an issue with an IC, where there's simply nothing we could do.

I had signed up to do just the first shift from 9:00 to 11:00, but the person that was going to take the second shift was sick so I ended up going until the end at 12:30. Despite the poor showing in regards to fixing things, I did have a good time and will, assuming scheduling works, volunteer at the next one.
